# Launch research (Step 0 protocol)

The starting state of a launch project determines which workflow steps dominate. Five modes; pick one before any other step.

## Mode detection

### Mode A (Pre-launch, greenfield)

Signals:

- No landing page, or only a placeholder ("Coming soon" with a waitlist form).
- No waitlist, or a Kit / Loops / Substack list of fewer than 50 addresses that has not been emailed.
- No prior launch activity on any public channel.
- No prior press coverage or influencer mention.
- Target launch date is in the future; the ask is "plan and execute the first launch."

Actions:

- Walk Step 1 through Step 12 in order.
- Spend the majority of effort on Step 1 (positioning) and Step 2 through Step 4 (landing page). Mode A launches succeed or fail on whether the hero sentence is specific.
- Launch channels (Step 8) typically include Product Hunt, Show HN, one or two subreddits, an X thread, a LinkedIn post, and an Indie Hackers milestone. Mode A launches skip the longer-lead-time press track (podcasts, monthly newsletters) unless the founder has existing relationships.

### Mode B (AI-slop landing exists)

Signals:

- A landing page is live, generated by Claude / Cursor / Lovable / v0 / Bolt / Framer AI or a combination.
- Hero sentence uses three or more banned words (seamless, powerful, revolutionary, effortless, intelligent, cutting-edge, game-changing, unlock, supercharge, streamline, empower, elevate, robust, best-in-class, leading, enterprise-grade, world-class).
- Feature grid has seven or more tiles or uses category-label headlines (Fast / Secure / Scalable).
- The OG card renders at 1200x630 but has not been previewed on LinkedIn, Slack, iMessage, or Discord.
- Visual identity is shadcn-default or Tailwind-starter: gradient hero, pastel icon blobs, three-column feature grid, Inter typography.

Actions:

- Run the substitution test (Step 1) against the existing hero, sub-hero, and feature headlines *first*. Record which sentences fail.
- Rewrite failed sentences before any other step. The visual identity can stay; the copy cannot.
- Run the banned-word grep across the full landing page content source (not just the rendered HTML; some frameworks inline copy in JSON or props).
- Audit the feature grid: if count exceeds six, cut to six or fewer. If each tile is a category label, rewrite as product-specific capabilities.
- Proceed through Steps 5 through 12 after the copy is clean. Do not ship the launch on the existing AI-slop copy.

### Mode C (Re-launch / pivot)

Signals:

- A prior launch happened. It did not necessarily fail; the product has shifted since.
- Positioning has changed (new audience, new category, new differentiator).
- Existing landing page reflects old positioning.
- Existing waitlist may include subscribers from the prior positioning; some will not recognize the new product.
- Prior press coverage and social posts are still findable.

Actions:

- Treat as Mode A for positioning and landing page work, with one addition: a legacy-messaging audit.
- The legacy-messaging audit: list every existing public artifact (prior landing page snapshot, press articles, PH page, Show HN thread, tweets, LinkedIn posts, IH milestones) and decide for each: update, remove, or mark-as-historical. Stale public artifacts that describe the old positioning will cannibalize the new launch's search impression if left.
- For the waitlist, draft a "we pivoted" email that goes out before the pre-launch sequence. Give subscribers an explicit unsubscribe ("if the new direction is not for you, here is a one-click unsub"). A churn of 30 to 60 percent is normal and healthy; the remainder is self-selected for the new positioning.
- Run the full Step 1 through Step 12 workflow on the new story.

### Mode D (Rescue / no traction)

Signals:

- A prior launch happened and returned to baseline within 48 hours.
- Total signups or waitlist joins from the launch were below the founder's threshold (typically under 100 for an indie product, under 1000 for a broader launch).
- Founder describes the launch as "flopped" or "silent."

Actions:

- Before any remediation, diagnose the dominant failure cause. The five launch-failure causes, in order of frequency:

  1. **Positioning.** Visitors arrived and bounced because the hero did not explain what the product does. The substitution test fails. This is the most common cause across AI-assisted launches in 2026.
  2. **Venue.** The launch was posted to the wrong channel or at the wrong time. PH on a Friday, Show HN at midnight PT (dead hour), Reddit to a sub with zero prior participation.
  3. **Amplification.** No hunter, no thread, no list, no asks sent in advance. The first hour had no activity; the algorithm did not pick it up.
  4. **Capture.** Visitors arrived but the waitlist form was broken, the double opt-in confirmation went to spam, or the CTA was buried. Analytics show landing page views but zero signups.
  5. **Timing.** The launch window coincided with a larger industry event (an Apple keynote, a TechCrunch Disrupt day, a major competitor's launch) that consumed attention.

- Diagnose using the analytics from the prior launch if available. Landing-page views but zero signups = capture. Low landing-page views despite broad posting = venue or amplification. High views, low signups, poor retention = positioning (visitors did not understand, so they did not sign up, so they did not activate).
- Write down the dominant cause explicitly before starting Step 1. The diagnosis is a one-paragraph document saved to `.launch-ready/retrospectives/<prior-launch>.md`.
- Remediate the dominant cause first; secondary causes second. Do not remediate all five at once in a re-launch; the changes compound and attribution of the next launch becomes impossible.

### Mode E (Quiet launch / B2B direct)

Signals:

- The product is B2B, typically mid-market or enterprise.
- The audience is reached through sales outreach, partnerships, and targeted content; not through broad launch channels.
- Product Hunt, Hacker News, and Reddit are not part of the plan (either irrelevant to the audience or actively counterproductive).
- The launch is about landing-page quality, positioning, and sales-enablement materials, not broadcast amplification.

Actions:

- Execute Step 1 through Step 7 fully. Positioning, landing page, SEO, OG cards, waitlist (if relevant; some B2B products use demo request forms instead) all matter.
- Skip or compress Step 8. Launch channels for Mode E are: LinkedIn founder-voice post, direct sales outreach to target accounts, an email to the existing business network. No PH / HN / Reddit.
- Execute Step 9. Press and outreach for Mode E is often the primary amplification: niche industry publications, analyst briefings, partner co-marketing.
- Execute Step 10, Step 11, Step 12 fully. Telemetry and runbook still matter; the channels differ.

## The launch-failure frequency heuristic

Anecdotal frequency from published founder postmortems and 2024 to 2026 indie-hacker retrospectives:

- Positioning failures are the most common cause of silent launches. The founder built something real; the hero sentence did not explain it.
- Venue and amplification failures are the second most common cause, often overlapping (wrong channel and no amplification together).
- Capture failures are rarer but more embarrassing: the form was broken, the confirmation email went to spam, the CTA was below the fold on mobile.
- Timing failures are real but usually overstated in retrospective; a strong launch survives a competing news day.

Launch-ready weights effort accordingly: positioning gets the most attention (Step 1, Step 3), capture gets strict checks (Step 7, Step 10), venue and amplification get structure (Step 8). Timing gets a single check at D-7 (major industry events on the target date) and no further focus.

## Common misdiagnoses

A few postmortem patterns that claim the wrong dominant cause:

- "We needed more upvotes" (attribution to amplification) when the landing page hero was actually indistinguishable from a competitor's (positioning). More upvotes would have brought more visitors and more bounces; the hero does not survive the traffic.
- "We launched on a Friday" (timing) when the real issue was that the Show HN title started with "Launch HN" and got flagged in four minutes (venue etiquette).
- "The product is too niche" (positioning of a different kind) when the launch posted to a general subreddit instead of the niche sub where the audience lives (venue mismatch).

The diagnosis must be specific enough to drive the remediation. "We need to launch better" is not a diagnosis.

## Resume protocol

On resume, read `.launch-ready/STATE.md` first. The STATE file names the mode, tier, and open questions. If STATE.md is missing, re-run the mode detection from the top of this reference; do not skip to Step 2.

If the mode has changed since the last session (for example, a Mode A project shipped a landing page between sessions and is now effectively Mode B), update the mode in STATE.md and re-run the substitution test against the new artifacts before continuing.
